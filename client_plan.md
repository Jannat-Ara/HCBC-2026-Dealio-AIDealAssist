# Client Plan: Autonomous AI Agent Deployment

## What the Client Wants

Build a **configurable, autonomous AI agent system** designed for small and medium enterprises (SMEs). The system adapts to any business — regardless of industry — and allows department heads and leadership to define objectives that the agents then execute end-to-end, with human sign-off at critical decision points.

---

## The Core Goal

Move from a company where humans do most of the work, to one where AI agents handle the bulk of operations and humans only step in for high-stakes decisions or exceptions.

| | Before | After |
|---|---|---|
| Human involvement | 70–80% of process work | Supervisory only |
| Routine task handling | Manual, siloed per department | Agents handle 80% autonomously |
| Decision-making | Requires human input at every step | Agents escalate only when needed |

---

## Who This Is For

**Primary audience: Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs)**

The system is not built for a single industry. It is a configurable platform — each business sets it up to match their own departments, workflows, and business rules. A logistics company, a clinic, and a financial firm would all run the same four agents, configured differently.

---

## The Four Core Agents

These agents work as a team, in sequence, triggered by leadership and executed across departments.

### 1. The Orchestrator
- Receives a business objective from a department head or C-suite executive
- Breaks the objective into actionable sub-tasks
- Coordinates and communicates with the other three agents throughout execution
- Acts as the central hub — nothing moves without passing through here

### 2. The Learner
- Checks the company's existing knowledge base before any action is taken
- Determines whether the requested task is feasible as-is
- Flags gaps, risks, or required adjustments
- Feeds its findings to the Decision Maker

### 3. The Decision Maker
- Takes the Learner's assessment and cross-references it with external inputs — market conditions, economic indicators, and applicable regulations
- Produces a structured decision report
- **Presents the report to a human decision-maker for approval before anything is executed**
- Nothing proceeds without human sign-off at this stage

### 4. The Task Generator
- Activated only after human approval
- Breaks the approved decision into specific, actionable tasks for each relevant department
- Ensures every team receives clear, department-appropriate instructions with no ambiguity

---

## How the Workflow Runs

```
Leadership sets objective
        ↓
  Orchestrator receives and coordinates
        ↓
  Learner checks knowledge base → flags feasibility
        ↓
  Decision Maker synthesizes + external data → produces report
        ↓
  Human reviews and approves
        ↓
  Task Generator issues department-specific tasks
```

---

## What the System Needs to Do

1. **Work across any business** — configurable for any industry or department structure, not locked to a specific sector
2. **Make decisions with context** — agents draw from internal knowledge and live external data, not just static rules
3. **Always involve a human at the critical moment** — the Decision Maker never acts unilaterally; a human approves before execution
4. **Stay secure and auditable** — every agent action, handoff, and decision is logged and traceable

---

## Deployment Approach

The system goes live within **24 hours**, following this phased plan:

| Phase | Timeframe | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| Strategy | Morning (0–4 hrs) | Define the first business objective and configure agent roles for the client |
| Setup | Midday (4–8 hrs) | Connect agents to the client's existing systems and knowledge base |
| Governance | Afternoon (8–12 hrs) | Set approval checkpoints, access controls, and audit logging |
| Launch | Evening (12+ hrs) | Test with real historical data, then go live |

---

## Key Business Outcomes Expected

- **60–80% reduction** in routine task handling across departments
- **88% reduction** in ongoing operational costs (vs. cloud-only approach)
- Full **data privacy** — sensitive business data stays in-house
- Leadership shifts from managing execution to **approving outcomes**
